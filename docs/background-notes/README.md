# NyxForge Background Notes

This directory contains research, design notes, and external summaries that inform the development of the NyxForge protocol. These notes are for informational purposes and may not reflect the final application implementation.

### 1. [horesh-spb-summary.md](horesh-spb-summary.md)
This file provides a comprehensive summary of Ronnie Horesh's 2011 book, *Market Solutions for Social and Environmental Problems: Social Policy Bonds*. It details the fundamental mechanism of outcome-based financial instruments, where redemption values are only paid upon the verified achievement of social goals. The summary highlights the importance of market-driven "bondholder coalitions," the design principles for setting verifiable and broad objectives, and the transition strategies from activity-based to outcome-based social policy.

### 2. [horesh-peacebonds-summary.md](horesh-peacebonds-summary.md)
A summary of Ronnie Horesh's 2003 book, *Market Incentives to End War: Conflict Reduction Bonds*. This document details the specific application of Social Policy Bonds to the problem of violent political conflict. It outlines how auctioning "Peacebonds"—which only pay out upon the verified, sustained reduction of casualties or proxy indicators—can bypass political gridlock and incentivize non-governmental actors to fund efficient, and sometimes unconventional, peace-building initiatives. It serves as the foundational text for NyxForge's intended "Peacebonds" application.

### 3. [anonymous-oracles.md](anonymous-oracles.md)
This document analyzes the landscape of open-source oracle networks with a focus on anonymity and commodity hardware compatibility. It notes the lack of a turnkey "Chainlink for Anons" and explores alternative building blocks such as TLS-Notary for Web2 data attestation and emerging zkTLS networks like Reclaim and Pado. The note also examines how NyxForge can leverage AO-native 0rbit while maintaining privacy through DarkFi's "Witness" model and Monero-based stealth address staking for skin-in-the-game enforcement.
